Project Description

This research paper delves into the realm of network intrusion detection, utilizing machine learning algorithms and the Luflow dataset. By categorizing network data into good and bad exploits, and employing techniques such as pre-processing and feature selection, the study aims to refine model precision. Notably, the decision tree and random forest classification algorithms exhibit superior accuracy, offering promising avenues for the development of more effective intrusion detection systems.

Through rigorous evaluation of hyperparameters derived from training and testing precision metrics, the research highlights the efficacy of machine learning in detecting and mitigating cybersecurity threats. With decision tree and random forest classifiers showcasing notable accuracy rates, the findings underscore the potential for enhanced security measures in dynamic network environments.
